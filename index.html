This project proposes the development of a cutting-edge Human Following Robot, specifically designed to autonomously assist the elderly in their daily activities—whether it's carrying their belongings, navigating environments, or providing essential support. By integrating advanced technologies like TensorFlow Lite and OpenCV for marker detection and human tracking, the robot will seamlessly follow its user while ensuring efficient interaction with its locomotion system. Equipped with cameras and sophisticated algorithms, the robot will be capable of recognizing and tracking humans, making it an invaluable aid in improving the quality of life for older adults.

The project covers every aspect of the development process, from the design to the coding, ensuring the robot is highly functional, safe, and reliable for elderly care. With an emphasis on innovation, the project will showcase how these technologies—coupled with practical applications—can revolutionize elder support systems.

Keywords: Human-following robot, human detection, human tracking, elderly care, TensorFlow Lite, OpenCV, cameras, algorithms, locomotion system.

PLC Programming

For this project, our team was assigned to design a program to handle a pick-and-place operation on the PLC bearing station. The task involves moving the equipment's arm from its initial middle position to the bearing, picking it up, and transferring it to a second position where the bearing is placed. Afterward, the arm smoothly returns to its starting point. If no bearing is detected, the arm stays put, even when the start button is pressed.

Additionally, the arm can shift from the middle to the back position if a bearing is detected there, pick it up, and move it to the front before resetting to the middle. At any time, pressing the stop button halts the operation, which can be resumed by pressing start. In manual mode, users need to press the start button for each step of the process.




By implementing SFC, managing sequential processes in a ladder diagram becomes much simpler. Plus, features like stopping or pausing the process are easier to integrate, as SFC can naturally hold a state by default.

ABB Robot
Programming

Pick-and-place operations are widely used in automated industrial processes. In this project, a four-person team developed a visually guided pick-and-place system using a robotic manipulator. The goal was to have the robot autonomously identify and manipulate different objects based on their size and position, ultimately rearranging them to form the word "HI."

The task involved recognizing three rectangular bars of the same length and one shorter bar placed in arbitrary positions. The robot had to accurately determine the size, position, and orientation of each bar and then rearrange them to spell out "HI." This required precise identification and manipulation to meet the projects goal.

The project was divided into several key tasks. First, forward and inverse kinematics were derived theoretically and implemented in MATLAB. The team then simulated the robot’s motion using the Robotics Toolbox. STL files of each part of the ABB IRB120 robot were extracted and combined in MATLAB to create a 3D model using robot.plot3d. In addition to modeling, image processing techniques were applied in MATLAB to distinguish between the different sizes of the bars and to locate their centroids. A RAPID program was written to simulate the robot’s pick-and-place operation based on this information. Finally, the position of the blocks in the target area was determined in MATLAB, and a virtual work environment was created in Robot Studio for further simulation and testing.




The Hand-Eye Coordination project, designed to detect objects of different sizes and rearrange them to form the word "HI," was a success. Two pick-and-place scenarios were implemented: one where no items were present in the assembly zone and another where objects were already within it. In the second scenario, the robot first moved the objects aside before rearranging them in the correct order. In the first scenario, the robot simply picked and placed the items as they did not interfere with the assembly area.

Traffic Light
Control System

This design exercise involved the creation of a controller for a traffic intersection, responsible for managing both the traffic and pedestrian walk lights. The intersection was designed with specific operational requirements. The roads were one-way streets where vehicles could only travel straight, with no turns allowed. Initially, the East-West traffic light remained green for an extended period. When a North-South car was detected, the traffic lights would cycle to green in that direction and hold for a short duration. The pedestrian walk light would only be activated if a pedestrian was detected either before the light turned green or during the hold period. After the short duration, if no car or pedestrian was detected on the East-West side, the green light remained for the North-South direction.

If a North-South pedestrian was detected, the traffic lights similarly cycled, and the appropriate walk light was displayed for a short interval. If no East-West car was detected, the North-South light remained green. In cases where cars were detected in both directions, the lights cycled for both directions, allowing each side the mandatory short duration. If a pedestrian wished to cross with traffic while a car or pedestrian was present on the opposite road, the lights cycled before granting the walk light to the first-mentioned pedestrian. Once the lights changed to green for a particular direction, they stayed green for a minimum time before another change was allowed. The lights remained green unless an input situation, such as the detection of a car or pedestrian, required a cycle. The design ensured that both directions displayed red for safety before switching to green for the other side.

The design also included a countdown timer, displaying the remaining time from green to amber or from red to green, ensuring drivers and pedestrians were aware of the timing of light changes. The intersection was symmetrical, meaning neither road had precedence, and the design principles were applied equally in both directions. Pedestrian buttons were designed to only be pressed briefly, as they would in a real-world scenario, while car detectors were held as long as the car remained in position, ensuring vehicles were only considered if they stayed long enough for the light change.

The project incorporated a timer with multiple outputs representing various time delays and a state machine to serve as the system's intelligence. The state machine controlled the timer and the lights, responding to the timer outputs, synchronized car buttons, and pedestrian buttons. The system was designed with safety considerations, ensuring that all inputs were handled correctly and efficiently.

Elixir Glasses
for the Visually Impaired

The Elixir smart glasses are designed to assist visually impaired individuals by providing features such as GPS navigation, echolocation, object proximity warning, and phone call assistance. These features aim to empower visually impaired users to navigate their surroundings independently and effectively.

The inspiration for creating Elixir stemmed from the desire to address the challenges faced by visually impaired people in their daily lives. The goal was to develop a product that would provide a sense of independence and make it easier for them to navigate their environment.

Elixir works by utilizing a combination of ultrasonic sensors, a camera, and an interactive voice assistant. The ultrasonic sensors detect obstacles, providing the user with information about their proximity. The camera allows users to connect with assistants who can provide spoken feedback and help identify objects or read documents. The voice assistant enables users to control incoming and outgoing calls, receive directions, and receive alerts when obstacles are detected.

In addition to these features, Elixir also includes a controller ring with three braille buttons that allow users to navigate the smart glasses' functions independently. These buttons are used to activate echolocation, voice command, and emergency assistance.

Overall, Elixir aims to provide visually impaired individuals with a valuable tool that enhances their independence and improves their ability to navigate their surroundings.
